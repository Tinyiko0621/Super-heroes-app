CAPTAIN AMERICA

From the dark days of World War II to the modern era, the Super-Soldier Captain America stands ready as a symbol of liberty—shielding the oppressed and fighting for freedom everywhere.

Ninety-Eight-Pound Weakling

Steve Rogers, a poor orphan from Brooklyn, was determined to fight the Axis powers but was rejected by the military due to his weak physique. Dr. Abraham Erskine saw potential in him and offered a chance through Project Rebirth—a secret Super-Soldier experiment.

Steve received the serum and radiation treatment, emerging stronger, faster, and more agile—becoming the world’s first Super-Soldier. Unfortunately, Erskine was assassinated, making Steve the only one of his kind.

Now a symbol of hope, Rogers trained and became Captain America—fighting behind enemy lines and inspiring troops. He gained a virtually indestructible shield, and over time faced enemies like the Red Skull, becoming a true legend of American heroism.
